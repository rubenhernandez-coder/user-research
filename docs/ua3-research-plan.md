## Target customer

- **Current claim:** Teenagers juggling academics and extracurricular obligations.
- **Questions:** How many high school students are actually juggling school plus outside commitments (sports, jobs, family responsibilities)? Is this a large enough group to build for?
- **Sources:** U.S. Census Bureau, Bureau of Labor Statistics, academic research (NIH/PMC), journalism
- **Enough:** 3 independent sources that agree on the general picture.
- **Expected findings:** Ruben predicted ~60% of students have something going on besides school.
- **Log:**
  - 2026-05-07 "percentage of high school students involved in extracurricular activities statistics" → Over 80% of middle and high school students participate in at least one extracurricular activity. [Census Bureau](https://www.census.gov/library/stories/2022/07/children-continue-to-be-involved-in-extracurricular-activities.html), [Mott Poll](https://mottpoll.org/reports/pay-participate-impact-school-activities). **Interpretation:** Higher than predicted. The target group is larger than expected.
  - 2026-05-07 "how many high school students work part time jobs statistics" → 22–30% of high school students hold part-time jobs depending on season; ~6 million teens aged 16–19 currently employed. [BLS](https://www.bls.gov/opub/ted/2024/employment-population-ratio-22-5-percent-for-high-school-students-44-3-percent-for-college-students.htm). **Interpretation:** Jobs add another layer on top of extracurriculars — many students deal with both.
  - 2026-05-07 "percentage of high school students juggling multiple commitments academics sports jobs time management 2024" → Student-athletes described feeling drained from constantly balancing time, lacking sleep, losing motivation. ~7 million high school student-athletes in the US. [Tandfonline](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13573322.2024.2338402). **Interpretation:** The pain is real and described in students' own words — not just a number but a feeling. Confirms the need clause too.
  - **Stopping criterion met.** Prediction was conservative (60%) — reality is 80%+. Clause holds as written.

## Need or opportunity

- **Current claim:** They struggle to balance their academic workload with everything else competing for their time.
- **Questions:** Does the struggle to balance time apply to all teenagers with outside commitments, or is it specific to student-athletes?
- **Sources:** Stress statistics databases, Mental Health America, academic journalism
- **Enough:** 1 search confirming the need is broader than athletes.
- **Expected findings:** Ruben predicted the struggle applies to all students juggling outside commitments, not just athletes.
- **Log:**
  - 2026-05-07 "high school students stress time management struggles balancing school work responsibilities statistics" → 75% of high school students feel constant stress over academic responsibilities. Only 30% of students feel they manage their time effectively. Heavy workloads lead to sleep deprivation and dropping other activities. [Cross River Therapy](https://www.crossrivertherapy.com/student-stress-statistics), [Mental Health America](https://mhanational.org/resources/balancing-work-and-school/), [Hillside Horizon](https://hillsidehorizon.com/blog/teens-academic-stress/). **Interpretation:** The struggle is not athlete-specific — it applies broadly to teenagers with heavy academic and outside commitments. Clause holds as written.
  - **Stopping criterion met.** Prediction confirmed. No revision to positioning statement needed.

## Product category

- **Current claim:** Smart study planner.
- **Questions:** Is "smart study planner" an established category? Have products like this been built before?
- **Sources:** App stores, product directories, web search for AI study planner tools
- **Enough:** Find one product that is identical or incorporates AI.
- **Expected findings:** Ruben predicted the combination of AI + planner + study tool was largely uncharted territory.
- **Log:**
  - 2026-05-07 "AI powered study planner app for students automatic schedule generator 2024 2025" → Multiple products exist: Scholarly, Trevor AI, StudyFetch, Canva study planner, kuse.ai, mymap.ai. Category is real and occupied. [Vertech Academy](https://www.vertechacademy.com/blog/best-ai-study-planner-apps-students), [Trevor AI](https://www.trevorai.com/use-cases/students), [Scholarly](https://scholarly.so/tools/study-schedule-generator). **Interpretation:** Prediction was wrong — the category exists and has real competition. "Smart study planner" is not a new category.
  - **Stopping criterion met.** Category is established. Differentiation clause needs sharpening — deferred to Pass 3 (interviews). Flagged as known gap.

## Primary competitive alternative

- **Current claim:** Trying to remember every deadline in your head, setting it as an alarm, or basic planner apps.
- **Questions:** Are teenagers actually using AI study planner apps, or are they relying on memory, alarms, and basic tools?
- **Sources:** Pew Research, Common Sense Media, app store search results
- **Enough:** 2 searches pointing in the same direction.
- **Expected findings:** Ruben predicted less than 10% of target students use a dedicated AI study planner.
- **Log:**
  - 2026-05-07 "high school students awareness usage AI study planner apps adoption rate teens 2024 2025" → Teens use AI broadly (ChatGPT for homework help) but no evidence of widespread AI study planner adoption specifically. Most AI usage is for assignments, not structured planning. [Pew Research](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2026/02/24/how-teens-use-and-view-ai/), [Programs.com](https://programs.com/resources/students-using-ai/). **Interpretation:** Broad AI adoption doesn't mean AI planner adoption — the specific category is likely low-awareness among 15–19 year olds.
  - 2026-05-07 "teenagers high school students using study planner apps vs paper calendar memory survey 2024" → Search returned mostly product listings for basic planner apps (MyStudyLife, myHomework) — no AI planners in top results. No survey data on actual student behavior found. [MyStudyLife](https://mystudylife.com/), [myHomework](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/myhomework-student-planner/id303490844). **Interpretation:** Basic planner apps are the dominant category students encounter, not AI planners. Clause revised to include basic planner apps alongside memory and alarms.
  - **Stopping criterion met.** Clause revised: added "basic planner apps" to the alternative. Status updated to drafted-unconfirmed — needs interview confirmation.

## Primary differentiation

- **Current claim:** Tells you how to use your time effectively and is built around the reality of a teenager's packed schedule.
- **Questions:** What do existing AI study planner and student planner apps lack? Is there a gap specifically for teenagers with packed schedules?
- **Sources:** App reviews, product comparison articles, user forums
- **Enough:** Find clear evidence of what existing products don't do that Study AI would do.
- **Expected findings:** Ruben predicted existing apps don't combine AI planning with a teenage-specific experience.
- **Log:**
  - 2026-05-07 "Scholarly Trevor AI study planner app reviews complaints missing features user feedback" → Trevor AI is a general productivity tool, not student-specific. Useful but aimed at professionals as much as students. Paywall limits full features. [AI Chief](https://aichief.com/ai-productivity-tools/trevor-ai/), [Trevor AI](https://www.trevorai.com/).  **Interpretation:** Trevor AI does auto-scheduling but isn't built for the teenage student's reality.
  - 2026-05-07 "MyStudyLife myHomework student planner app reviews what students wish it could do limitations" → MyStudyLife and myHomework are passive trackers — they record deadlines but don't tell students when to work. Interfaces feel dated. Manual entry required. No intelligence behind scheduling. [CourseSyn](https://www.coursesync.biz/post/myhomework-student-planner-review-is-it-still-worth-using), [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/my-study-life-school-planner/id910639339). **Interpretation:** Student-specific apps don't plan — they just track. The gap Study AI fills appears real.
  - **Stopping criterion met.** Differentiation clause revised to reflect the gap: active planning built specifically for teenagers, not passive tracking or professional tools. Status updated to drafted-unconfirmed — needs interview confirmation. Known gap resolved from status file.
